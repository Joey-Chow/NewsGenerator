Gold and silver prices surged to record highs on Monday, while European share markets registered sharp declines, following new tariff threats from US President Donald Trump.

Gold touched $4,689.39 an ounce, with silver rising to $94.08 an ounce, as investors sought safe-haven assets amidst escalating geopolitical uncertainty. President Trump announced a 10% tariff on goods from eight European nations, including Germany, France, and the UK. These tariffs, set to begin February 1st and potentially rising to 25%, are linked to opposition over his proposed takeover of Greenland.

European indices reacted negatively. London's FTSE 100 closed down almost 0.4%, Germany's Dax index fell 1.3%, and France's Cac 40 dropped 1.8%. Carmakers and luxury goods firms across the continent experienced significant falls. The European Union is reportedly considering a €93 billion package of retaliatory tariffs. The International Monetary Fund has previously warned that trade tensions pose a significant risk to global economic growth.
